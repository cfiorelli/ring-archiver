Ring Video Archiver

Save and keep your Ring videos — right on your own Mac.

Before you start, have these handy

  • Your Ring email and password
  • Your phone (Ring will text you a code)
  • Enough free space on your Mac (or a plugged-in external drive) — there can be a lot of videos
1

Download the app

Click the button that matches your Mac. Not sure which? Click the Apple menu (top-left) → About This Mac: if it says “Chip: Apple…” choose Apple Silicon; if it says “Intel”, choose Intel.

Using a Windows PC or Linux instead?
2

Open the downloaded file

Look in your Downloads folder. You'll see a file that ends in .zip. Double-click it once — it unzips into an app called RingArchiver.

(If you don't see the app, the zip may be in a folder with the same name — open that folder to find RingArchiver.)

3

Let your Mac open it (first time only)

Because this is a small free tool, your Mac will block it the very first time. This is normal. Pick either way below — you only do this once.

Way A — no typing (easiest)

1. Double-click RingArchiver. A message says it can't be opened — click Done.
2. Open the Apple menu → System Settings → Privacy & Security.
3. Scroll down to the message about RingArchiver and click Open Anyway, then confirm.

Way B — one quick command

Open the Terminal app (press ⌘+Space, type Terminal, press Return). Click Copy, paste it in (⌘+V), press Return — then double-click RingArchiver.

xattr -dr com.apple.quarantine ~/Downloads/RingArchiver.app

This just tells your Mac the app is safe. It downloads nothing and changes nothing else.

4

Sign in to Ring

The app opens a page in your web browser by itself. Type your Ring email and password. Ring will text a 6-digit code to your phone — type that in too. (You only sign in once.)

5

Choose your camera and start

Pick your camera (for example “Diane's room”), leave it on Everything available, and choose where to save (the Ring Videos folder is fine, or your external drive). Then click Download videos.

It saves the oldest videos first and shows a progress bar. There may be a LOT, so it can run a long time — that's okay. You can close it and reopen anytime; it remembers what's already saved and keeps going.
6

You're done

When it says Complete ✓, all your videos are in the Ring Videos folder, sorted by month, with a list file (manifest.csv) of everything saved.

Everything stays on your own Mac. Your videos and password never go anywhere except to Ring itself.

🌿 Made with care. If a step ever looks stuck, just close the app and open it again — it picks up right where it left off.
